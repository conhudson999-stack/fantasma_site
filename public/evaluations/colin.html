WHAT YOU DO GREAT.

POWERFUL SHOT

For 9 years old, Colin has a great shot. He has an impressive ability to get a lot of power behind the ball, and that's something that doesn't come around often at his age. AREAS TO DEVELOP.

These aren't weaknesses — they're opportunities. Every great player has areas they're actively working to improve.

01

SHOT PLACEMENT & PRECISION

Since Colin already has a powerful shot, the next progression in his development is learning to place it exactly where he wants. We worked on this during our first session — low driven, purposeful shots. The power is there; now it's about honing that power so he can pick a spot in the net and put it there consistently.

How to improve: Practice shooting at specific targets — pick a corner or a spot along the ground and see how many times in a row you can hit it. Start close, then move back. The goal is to make that powerful shot do exactly what you want it to do.
02

QUICKNESS OF FEET

The next area of focus is the overall quickness of Colin's feet. I want to work with him on continuously being on his toes, in an athletic stance, ready for the next ball. Quick, hot feet form a strong foundation for the rest of the game — whether it's dribbling, defending, or reacting to loose balls.

How to improve: We'll work on this together through drills in tight areas, ladder work, small hurdles, and different types of plyometrics. These exercises build the fast-twitch muscle and foot speed that translate directly to every part of the game.
03

OVERALL COMFORT WITH THE BALL

I would say this to just about any player Colin's age. At this stage, the most important thing is getting comfortable with the ball using different parts of the foot in different scenarios. It's about trial and error — if he plays with the ball on his own, he'll discover that this part of the foot does this, that part of the foot does that. Soon those lessons become subconscious, and that's when the game gets really fun — when a player isn't thinking about how to do something, they're just thinking about what they want to do.

How to improve: More touches on the ball in any form — dribbling around the yard, juggling, playing with friends. The goal is volume. The more situations he sees the ball in, the faster his instincts develop.
03

HOMEWORK DRILLS.

Targets: Shot placement / Precision

TARGET SHOOTING

Take time practicing precise shots. Go to an open net, pick out a specific spot, and see how many times you can put it there in a row. Colin already has the powerful shot — now it's about making that power do exactly what he wants. Start close and work your way back, focusing on hitting the same target every time.

Targets: Foot speed / Agility

PLYOMETRICS & FOOTWORK

This is something Colin and I will work on together in our sessions — ladders, hurdles, and different types of plyometric exercises designed to build quick, explosive feet. Between sessions, any kind of movement that gets him on his toes and reacting quickly will help reinforce what we're building.

Targets: Comfort on the ball / Touch control

JUGGLING

Juggling is huge for developing a great first touch and getting comfortable with the ball using different surfaces of the foot. Start with one juggle and a catch, then two and a catch, and build from there. No pressure on how many — the goal is just getting more comfortable with the ball off the ground.

Targets: Comfort on the ball / All parts of foot

BALL MASTERY DRILLS

Look up "soccer ball mastery drills" on YouTube — there are tons of great follow-along videos. These are drills Colin can do in a very small space that focus on quick touches using every surface of the foot. Even 10–15 minutes a few times a week will make a noticeable difference in how comfortable he feels with the ball.
